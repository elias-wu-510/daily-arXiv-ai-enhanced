### [1] [Memorization In Stable Diffusion Is Unexpectedly Driven by CLIP Embeddings](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.02908)
*Bumjun Kim,Albert No*

Main category: cs.CV

TL;DR: This paper discovers that in Stable Diffusion, pad embeddings (v^pad) unintentionally amplify the end-of-text embedding (v^eot), causing memorization. The authors propose two simple inference-time fixes: replacing the tokenizer's default pad token and/or partially masking pad embeddings to suppress memorization without sacrificing generation quality.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: To understand and mitigate memorization in text-to-image diffusion models by analyzing how specific CLIP textual embeddings contribute to this phenomenon, improving both interpretability and safety.

Method: The authors categorize CLIP embeddings into four types (sot, pr, eot, pad), analyze their contributions to memorization, and propose two inference-time mitigation strategies: (1) replacing the default <pad> token from <eot> to ! and masking v^eot; (2) partially masking v^pad.

Result: The pr embeddings contribute minimally to generation in memorized cases. The pad embeddings strongly affect memorization due to structural duplication of eot, amplifying its influence. Both proposed strategies suppress memorization effectively without degrading generation quality.

Conclusion: Memorization in Stable Diffusion is driven by unintended amplification of eot embeddings through pad embeddings. Simple token-level interventions at inference time can mitigate this issue without requiring prior detection or retraining.

Abstract: Understanding how textual embeddings contribute to memorization in text-to-image diffusion models is crucial for both interpretability and safety. This paper investigates an unexpected behavior of CLIP embeddings in Stable Diffusion, revealing that the model disproportionately relies on specific embeddings. We categorize input tokens as <startoftext>, <prompt>, <endoftext> and <pad> with corresponding embeddings $\mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{sot}}, \mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{pr}}, \mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{eot}}, \mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{pad}}$. We discover that $\mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{pr}}$ contribute minimally to generation in memorized cases. In contrast, $\mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{pad}}$ strongly affect memorization due to their structural duplication of $\mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{eot}}$, the only embedding explicitly optimized during CLIP training. This duplication unintentionally amplifies the influence of $\mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{eot}}$, causing the model to over-rely on it, thereby driving memorization. Based on these observations, we propose two simple yet effective inference-time mitigation strategies: (1) Replacing the tokenizer's default <pad> from <eot> to the ! token before embedding, and masking the $\mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{eot}}$; (2) Partial masking of $\mathbf{v}^{\mathbf{pad}}$. Both suppress memorization without degrading quality, and are readily deployable without prior detection.
