### [1] [Ranking-Guided Semi-Supervised Domain Adaptation for Severity Classification](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01834)
*Shota Harada,Ryoma Bise,Kiyohito Tanaka,Seiichi Uchida*

Main category: cs.CV

TL;DR: A novel semi-supervised domain adaptation method for medical severity classification that uses ranking with class order to align domains through cross-domain ranking and continuous distribution alignment.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Existing semi-supervised domain adaptation methods struggle with severity classification in medical images due to unclear class boundaries and the naturally ordered nature of severity labels, making domain adaptation challenging.

Method: Proposes a method that aligns source and target domains using rank scores learned via ranking with class order. Two key components: 1) Cross-Domain Ranking ranks sample pairs across domains, and 2) Continuous Distribution Alignment aligns rank score distributions between domains.

Result: Experiments on ulcerative colitis and diabetic retinopathy classification validate the effectiveness of the approach, demonstrating successful alignment of class-specific rank score distributions between domains.

Conclusion: The proposed ranking-based approach effectively addresses the challenges of severity classification in semi-supervised domain adaptation for medical image analysis by leveraging the ordinal nature of severity labels.

Abstract: Semi-supervised domain adaptation leverages a few labeled and many unlabeled target samples, making it promising for addressing domain shifts in medical image analysis. However, existing methods struggle with severity classification due to unclear class boundaries. Severity classification involves naturally ordered class labels, complicating adaptation. We propose a novel method that aligns source and target domains using rank scores learned via ranking with class order. Specifically, Cross-Domain Ranking ranks sample pairs across domains, while Continuous Distribution Alignment aligns rank score distributions. Experiments on ulcerative colitis and diabetic retinopathy classification validate the effectiveness of our approach, demonstrating successful alignment of class-specific rank score distributions.

### [2] [Semantic Segmentation of Textured Non-manifold 3D Meshes using Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.01836)
*Mohammadreza Heidarianbaei,Max Mehltretter,Franz Rottensteiner*

Main category: cs.CV

TL;DR: Texture-aware transformer for semantic segmentation of 3D meshes that leverages both geometric and textural information through hierarchical learning and two-stage transformer blocks.


<details>
  <summary>Details</summary>
Motivation: Existing mesh segmentation methods either ignore texture information or impose geometric constraints, failing to fully utilize the rich appearance data available in textured 3D meshes.

Method: Proposes a texture-aware transformer with hierarchical learning: 1) Texture branch summarizes face-level pixels into learnable tokens, 2) Fuses with geometric descriptors, 3) Processes through Two-Stage Transformer Blocks (TSTB) for local and global information flow, 4) Multi-scale feature aggregation.

Result: Achieves 81.9% mF1 and 94.3% OA on Semantic Urban Meshes (SUM) benchmark, and 49.7% mF1 and 72.8% OA on new cultural-heritage dataset with textured roof tiles, substantially outperforming existing approaches.

Conclusion: The proposed texture-aware transformer effectively leverages both geometric and textural information for semantic segmentation of 3D meshes, demonstrating superior performance on urban and cultural-heritage datasets.

Abstract: Textured 3D meshes jointly represent geometry, topology, and appearance, yet their irregular structure poses significant challenges for deep-learning-based semantic segmentation. While a few recent methods operate directly on meshes without imposing geometric constraints, they typically overlook the rich textural information also provided by such meshes. We introduce a texture-aware transformer that learns directly from raw pixels associated with each mesh face, coupled with a new hierarchical learning scheme for multi-scale feature aggregation. A texture branch summarizes all face-level pixels into a learnable token, which is fused with geometrical descriptors and processed by a stack of Two-Stage Transformer Blocks (TSTB), which allow for both a local and a global information flow. We evaluate our model on the Semantic Urban Meshes (SUM) benchmark and a newly curated cultural-heritage dataset comprising textured roof tiles with triangle-level annotations for damage types. Our method achieves 81.9\% mF1 and 94.3\% OA on SUM and 49.7\% mF1 and 72.8\% OA on the new dataset, substantially outperforming existing approaches.

</details>
